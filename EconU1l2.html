Where it Began

Take a step back. How did we discover gravity? Isaac Newton was theorizing the physical nature of gravity hundreds of years ago and discovered gravity when he was hit on the head with an apple while sitting under a tree.

Does gravity exist everywhere? Experiments later verified that it does, but experiments also verified that gravity is different at high altitudes, sea level, and in space.

Purpose of Experiments

The purpose of an experiment is to test a hypothesis (an explanation or idea for why something happens or works) to see if it is true or not. Based on the experiment, the data is collected and reviewed and conclusions are formed. A hypothesis must be tested multiple times, by different scientists and different conditions in order to become a theory.

Models

A model is a way to display abstract, often complex data and represent it in a logical and digestible way. Often times this is done visually through schematics and graphs or through mathematical formulas.

The Scientific method

We've already discussed the development of theories and models from these theories. The process of experimentation is done in such a way as to allow other researchers the opportunity to replicate the results of prior researchers. This is called the Scientific Method. The graphic just to our left illustrates this method.

The scientific method is a process of experimentation done in such a way as to allow other researchers the opportunity to replicate the results.

Process of the Scientific Method

Observation

An idea,theory,problem or supposition should be formulated as a question to be addressed through a well-defined process in an attempt to prove or disprove the issue.

RESEARCH

Research the subject and observe what could be the cause or causes of the problem and identify or develop a number of solutions.

HYPOTHESIS

Outline the best options from your observations or past experiences or previous reports and make an educated guess about the cause and effect of a particular situation or set of factors that can be tested and proven right or wrong.

THINK!

Evaluate and think about the issue again, do some more research and start over with a new hypothesis.

EXPERIMENT

Social sciences such as economics must rely on data gathered from human subjects, such as from interviews,surveys or direct observation of the human participants in a controlled and sometimes uncontrolled environment or set of circumstances. These tests, surveys and experiments must be repeated with a number of other groups to verify findings.

ANALYSIS

Summarize your results and conclusions representing them in a number of ways such as a graph, charts, and written reports. Be sure to refer back to your data and observations.

HYPOTHESIS IS TRUE

Do the results support the experimental hypothesis? If so, some of the conclusions reached may be the answer to the problem.

COMMUNICATE

Discuss how the results apply to “real world” issues and indicate any other experiments that may be conducted to support the conclusions given in the report.

HYPOTHESIS IS FAKE

Unsuccessful experiments provide information that can lead to solutions by eliminating certain options that are not beneficial to resolving the problem.

Steps in the Process

  • Does smoking cause cancer? Is poverty caused by a lack of education? How much should the Federal Reserve cut inflation rates in order to stimulate economic growth?
  • A good researcher will conduct some background research and develop a hypothesis, a detailed opinion, to put his or her question into more focus.” “Smoking two packs of cigarettes per day for 25 years may cause cancer in 80% of the population. Failure to complete the 10th grade increases the probability that one will be poor by 90%
  • When the Federal Reserve cuts interest rates by more than 10% from previous levels, economic growth is stifled. Do you see how these statements are very specific? Researchers turn these statements into tailored questions to permit formal research to take place, hoping to develop proof to support their claims.
  • For example, instead of stating that smoking two packs of cigarettes per day for 25 years may cause cancer in 80% of the population, ask "Does smoking two packs of cigarettes per day for 25 years cause cancer in 80% of the population?

    • Two Important Points about
    Conducting Research

    Two things are most important to be mindful of when conducting research. First, we must delineate WHAT SHOULD BE with WHAT IS. Second, scientific reporting should always be ethical. Sometimes in their zeal to advance a particular point of view or opinion, researchers do not report accurate findings.

    Should vs. Is

    In regard to the first point: What’s the difference between the words "should" and "is?" "Should" explains the way things would be in an ideal state. Just because things should be one way or another does not mean they are. "Is" explains how things actually are. Every subject discipline has its own lingo or code words, and within economic lingo, statements that say what should be are called normative statements. Statements that say what is are called positive statements.

    Unbiased Results

    Even though researchers try to eliminate bias from their research, it is possible that a study may be biased without the researcher realizing. That is why it is important to be able to identify types of bias and how to avoid them. There are many types of bias, including systematic bias, selection bias, and confirmation bias. Watch this short video on how to avoid bias in research. In your notes, write down the types of bias mentioned and ways they can be avoided. Then think about this-- why do you think it is important that research results are unbiased?

    False Evidence

    In 2007, a Korean researcher falsely claimed to have developed a way to clone human beings. In the 1980s, scientists claimed to develop a way to initiate cold fusion, a type of extremely efficient chemical reaction. No matter the personal agenda, if scientific findings are misstated, we as a species are irreparably harmed in evolving intellectually. Real science should be able to be replicated so that science evolves to develop discoveries and inventions that improve lives.
